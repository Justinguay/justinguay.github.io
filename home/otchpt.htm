    David woke at 7:00 AM on a chilly winter morning.  The apartment was dimly lit by the morning sun, but David’s eyes were still cloudy with the remnants of sleep as he made his way to the bathroom to get ready for work.

            A hot shower and a cup of coffee refreshed his spirit soon enough, and David took a moment to read the book he had received from Dr. Ketler.  Now that he was out of the metaphysics research facility he found that he had more time for such pleasantries.

            Both he and Lucien had been returned to their prior research projects for a much needed change of pace.  The metaphysics institute had decided that the heavy demands of the CAIN research program would be too straining over time, and a furlough was ordered.  The men were quite glad to get an escape from the attention, even if they still had to work at their old jobs.  Fortunately no one really knew what they had done during there many months of absence, and most weren’t terribly concerned either, not being metaphysicists themselves.

            David and Lucien had begun to maintain a consistent correspondence, sharing their experiences with the CAIN implants.  One of the other goals of the furlough was to test and expand each subject’s new analytical abilities in the practical applications of the lab.

To this aim the men applied themselves readily.  They were learning to see more and more with the new insights that their implants gave them.  Hurdles that had held their projects up for months during their absence seemed simple to overcome with the proper application of metaphysics.  The men were met with great success in every venture that they tried.

Of course careful tabs were kept of each man by the metaphysics institute as well.  Weekly reports were sent in, and monthly exams were a requirement.  Since David and Lucien had first seen the anomaly in the medical center neurological and psychological evaluations had become a must as well.

To both David and Lucien’s surprise the anomaly that they had seen was not an unprecedented event.  There was even a name for the mathematical anomalies.  The aberrations in chaos had been labeled ‘Macrobes’ but their existence was still heavily contested. 

Macrobes were first postulated in the early years after metaphysics had become an established science, although at the time the concept was not taken seriously.  The theory essentially postulated that the patterns of life were not limited to the confines of biological matter.  Even in biology it was seen that a pattern was taken from its core medium, the DNA of the first embryonic cell, and propagated into an alternate expression of the same original pattern in a different medium, that of the adult organism.  Pattern propagation of this kind is seen in many areas.  The pattern of words on a page can be reflected as signals received by the eye and sent to the brain, directed to the muscles of the mouth, and repeated again by means of sound vibrations.  Throughout each step the essence of the pattern remains constant on the metaphysical level, even though its medium changes several times.  Nearly all things can carry information, sound, light, even the movement of a branch in the wind as was seen by Lucien and David. These concepts allowed for the possible existence of patterns capable of propagating themselves through any kind of information carrying medium. 

Unfortunately evidence for the existence of such “self propagating patterns” was few and far between.  The few experiments designed to test for the existence of such entities were successful in finding only hints and clues, but nothing that could persuade the scientific community.  Lucien and David experienced in this criticism when their first reports of seeing a macrobe were met with extensive neurological and psychological examinations. 

Fortunately the directors of the metaphysics institute were of an open minded sort.  Suggestions had been made that the project should be terminated.  Such a measure would necessitate more dangerous surgery to remove the cybernetic implants which could not longer be simply deactivated because of their artificial intelligence self programming features.  The decision was made by the board, however, in light of the circumstantial evidence suggesting the existence of macrobes, to continue the project.  The compromise was that there would be regular and in-depth examinations made for the safety of both of the test subjects. 

David remembered how tense some of those meetings had been.  He certainly did not want to go back under the knife remembering what had happened the last time.  Surprisingly though, the man whom he had come to recognize as the wisest on the metaphysics research board, Dr. Ketler had remained gravely silent and reflective during the meetings.  He seemed deep in thought through most of the meetings and didn’t have much to contribute. 

This was a disappointment to David as he had hoped to get to know the Doctor better, and learn more about this new way of thinking he had found in the medical center after his surgery.  David remembered well his experiences after the implant surgery, and after talking with Dr. Ketler the distorted details of his vision became much clearer.  David wasn’t sure what to do with the ideas that floated around in his head; all he knew was that he could no longer avoid the question of ultimate reality.  Dr. Ketler’s words by his hospital bed and later the few conversations that they had shared amidst their busy schedules confirmed the feelings he had been inwardly wrestling with for so long, and provided a valid and believable philosophy to explain them.

He knew that he now believed in something, “There has to be a purpose,” he thought.  But even as he read the tattered old study book loaned to him by Dr. Ketler he could not figure out what he should do about it.  He wished that he could have talked with Dr. Ketler to find the answers and ease the struggle in his mind, but the older man had fallen ill and David didn’t want to bother him.  His mind was full of tension; he was excited and relieved to finally have accepted that there was purpose and meaning to his life, and that maybe even there was a creator somewhere looking down on him.  But the world seemed like such a cold place to any kind of firm belief much less a theology.   He couldn’t even share it with his best friend Lucien.  His heart was heavily burdened with the desire to talk about what he had found, but fearful of what would result.

David closed the book and ran his fingers through his short dark hair which had grown back some since the surgery.   He reminded himself that he had to get to work, and that he needed to focus.  “I have a big day ahead of me,” he said under his breath.

David gathered his things and made his way down to the company car he used to commute to the Research Institute of Temporal Studies.  It didn’t take him long to get there, and once at work he found it easier to get his mind off of the troubles and onto his work.  Walking to his office, a young woman, one of the assistants met him with a report of readings received from a recent experiment. 

“The pod has been overheating,” she said handing David a printout full of temperature readings from a recent time dilation experiment as she continued, “I’m not sure what the problem is.”

David looked at the print out, the last few readings were way off scale for acceptable parameters.  Keeping the unit cool under the highly energetic environment required for time dilation was one of the greatest struggles that the project was facing.  Looking at the page David began to scan through the numbers.  As he did his cybernetic implant silently went to work formulating mathematical visions on the page for him. 

“It’s a valve,” he said.

“What?” asked his co-worker, bewildered at his confident reply.

“A valve, see here,” David pointed to the numbers on the page, “The temperature starts fluctuating regularly after 220o, it’s a purely mechanical failure.”

David handed the page back to the astonished woman.  “But how did you...” she stuttered as David walked into his office.

David draped his grey trench coat over the back of his chair.  There was a letter on his desk.  He picked it up and found that it was from Lucien.  “Good!” he thought, “I haven’t heard from him in a few weeks now.”

Over the past few weeks, he and Lucien had been conversing via letters.  They had talked about new ways they were finding uses for their CAIN implants, and different ways of using them, but lately the correspondence had been mainly about the macrobes.  As their skills increased they began to see more and more of them, and see how they were functioning in the environment around them.  It was unsettling to see how frequently they appeared in the chaos of events and systems.  It seemed that sometimes they even were affecting the chaos and random events.

David opened the letter, but before he was able to read an intern came asking for his assistance.  From there his day was off, first he assisted in locating the faulty valve on the new temporal dilator, and redesigned a new one mathematically in-tune with the needed function, and environment.

Soon after though, he had to move on to his major task for the day.  In his absence time dilation had been advanced for enough to allow living things to undergo the experience.  However, because of the severe distortions created between the “normal” time and expanded time, little information could be gained from such experiments.   They were still thought to be valuable regardless of this fact because it was expected that time dilation was the first step moving towards a sort of time travel.  Having returned to find the procedure refined enough for living organism, David had been curious, to discover, what could be seen through the temporal distortion of the time dilator with the aid of his cybernetic implant.  He had sent out the request forms immediately and after proving that the procedure was safe and that the possible discoveries extremely valuable been approved to undergo the procedure himself.

Now the time had come.  The experiment was to be preformed on the following day.  In preparations David examined the equipment to insure that it would function properly.  His CAIN device even helped with this as he was able to mathematically estimate spring coefficients, tensile strength, and other properties that had to be finely tuned for the procedure to work.  He also had taken on the task of ensuring that the pod carrying him was tuned exactly to his own body, his weight, density, size, everything had to be taken into account, but thanks again to his implant the process became as simple as walking about and looking at the different components of the apparatus.

Finally at the end of the long day David returned to his office to collect his things.  He was surprised to find himself nervous about the upcoming experiment.  His apprehension must be due to the last experiment he reasoned.  David reflected on his feelings of uneasiness before undergoing the surgery grafting the chip into his brain, and the vision it had created as Dr. Ketler spoke, while his life was hanging in the balance.  So much had changed since before that time, and there was so much to think about.

David looked down at his desk and saw the half opened letter from Lucien.  He pulled the letter out and sat at his desk to read.

 

“Dear David,

 

Sorry for having not gotten back to you sooner buddy, but there have been some very interesting events here.  The macrobes have become clearer and clearer to see, as you know and have also experienced.  I still find it ironic how the CAIN interprets their mathematical forms as recognizable shapes and things from this world, like the face we saw the first time.  It’s funny how metaphysics relates things together at from level of size and complexity. 

David something incredible happened, or almost happened and I had to tell you right away so that you could be prepared if it happened to you as well.

 I have begun meditating in order to focus when I encountered a macrobe.  While doing this the other this morning with a macrobe I encountered working in the underlying patters given off by the radiator in my apartment (of all things!) something different happened.  Every macrobe encountered until now had seemed to escape as soon as it was detected.  This time it didn’t move.  The representation generated by my implant looked similar to a symbol I had seen in a book.  As I watched the macrobe faded and I thought it would disappear, but then it returned unexpectedly.  When it returned the second time, something uncanny happened, the normal hisses and clangs of the old radiator began to organize and then I heard it.  “amend” it was clear as day.  The only problem is that I have no idea what it meant. 

David, we had never before considered it but what if these macrobes, these patterns within patterns could be sentient.  It said a word, or caused a word, and in English, I just wish that I knew what it meant.  Was it making a fixing something, am I to change something, or was it some form of parroting and holds no meaning at all.

Either way one thing is clear, this was something unprecedented; the sound was audible without my CAIN unit.  I’m going to continue investigating, but I wanted to tell you so that you could keep your eyes open for the same thing.

 

I’ll write again soon,

 

Lucien

David looked up from reading the letter.  Could these anomalies be complex enough to be sentient?  I t was too much to think about.  His mind already weary with a long day’s work, and stress over the upcoming events was too bewildered to consider the implications seriously.

He put his trench coat on, slid the letter into the inside pocket, and sighed deeply.  Making his way out of the office, he felt disoriented, and confused.  It seemed like every day since joining the CAIN project the world had become a stranger and stranger place.

On his way out David stopped by his favorite place in the research center, the animal lab.  He came to a large cage housing a chimpanzee and reached his hand inside.  “Hey Corvus, how are you doing tonight.”

With little attention to David the ape reached for his hand and turned it to see his chrome watch fascinated with the object.

“It’s going to be ok isn’t it big guy,” David said softly.  He wasn’t sure if he was reassuring the chimp or himself.  He had visited the chimp several times since being authorized for the time dilation experiment.  Corvus, named for her exceptionally black hair, had been one of the first living animals to undergo the time dilation procedure.  David felt reassured seeing the chimp doing well having undergone the experiment which he was about to undertake for himself. 

“You behave girl,” David gently ordered, “I’ll be back tomorrow, and we’ll see what you saw when they plugged you into the machine.”
